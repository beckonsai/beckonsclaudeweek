Where to find Claude

Products guide

Claude lives in a few different places. Here's what each one is for, and which ones Beckons uses day-to-day.

Claude Chat

💬

claude.ai — your everyday starting point

Open a new conversation and just start typing. Great for one-off tasks: drafting an email, summarising a document, getting a quick answer, or brainstorming ideas. Think of it like a very capable colleague who never gets tired of questions.

Best for: ad hoc tasks · exploring ideas · quick answers · drafting

Projects

📁

Projects — persistent context for ongoing work

A Project is a dedicated workspace that remembers context across conversations. You can upload documents, set a system prompt (custom instructions), and all chats within that project share the same background knowledge. Perfect for ongoing workstreams.

Best for: recurring workflows · team projects · document-heavy tasks

Example — Reservations Project
"I've set up a Project for the Reservations team with our rate plans, property FAQs, and tone of voice guide uploaded. Every chat in this project automatically knows all of that."
→ No need to re-paste documents every time. Claude remembers.

Skills

⚡

Skills — one-click workflows

Skills are repeatable, pre-configured workflows built for specific tasks. Instead of writing the same prompt every time, a Skill packages it up so anyone can run it in one click. Think of it as a macro for AI tasks.

Example Skills at Beckons: Daily Guest Manifest · EDM drafting · Rate plan summaries

Design & Artifacts

🎨

Design / Artifacts — create documents and visuals

Claude can produce downloadable files — Word documents, Excel spreadsheets, PDFs, slide decks, images, HTML pages and more. When Claude creates a file, you'll see a download button appear. Great for producing polished outputs without formatting them yourself.

Best for: reports · templates · formatted outputs · visual summaries

Code — not for general use

Restricted — IT approval required
🖥️

Claude Code — developer tool only

Claude Code is a command-line tool designed for writing and running software code. It connects directly to your computer's file system and can execute commands. This is a powerful technical tool that should only be used by approved team members with a clear technical need and IT sign-off.

Not for general business use. Please do not install without speaking to your manager and IT first.

Choosing the right model

Sonnet, Opus & Haiku

Claude comes in three versions — think of them as different gears. Each one is optimised for a different balance of speed, depth, and cost. When you're on claude.ai, you can choose which model to use from the conversation selector.

ModelBest forSpeedDepth
Claude OpusMost powerfulComplex reasoning, M&A analysis, multi-step financial modelling, long-document synthesis, nuanced strategic workSlowerDeepest
Claude SonnetRecommended defaultThe best all-rounder — handles almost everything well. Drafting, analysis, summarising. Use this for 90% of your daily tasks.FastStrong
Claude HaikuFastestSimple, quick tasks: short replies, formatting, basic Q&A, repetitive processing where speed matters more than depthFastestGood
Which should I use at Beckons? Start with Sonnet for everything — it's fast, capable, and handles the vast majority of business tasks excellently. Switch to Opus when you need deep analysis: complex financial models, M&A screening, lengthy due diligence documents, or anything where you want Claude to think hard and not cut corners. Use Haiku for high-volume, simple tasks where you want near-instant responses.

When to switch models — quick guide

  • Stick with Sonnet (default)Drafting emails, summarising documents, writing SOPs, analysing short data sets, guest communications, marketing copy, meeting prep, and most day-to-day work.
  • Upgrade to OpusM&A target screening and due diligence synthesis · Underwriting and ROI analysis · Complex multi-document analysis · Board-level strategic memos · Anything where you need Claude to reason carefully through ambiguous or high-stakes material. If the output really matters, reach for Opus.
  • Drop to HaikuQuickly reformatting a table · Translating a short passage · Generating a list of options · Simple classification tasks · Any task where the output is simple and you want it instantly.
Model selection in practice
"I need to draft a quick reply to a guest enquiry about Silky Oaks availability."
→ Sonnet is perfect. Fast and polished.
"I need to build a screening memo for a potential acquisition target in Africa, cross-referencing their financials against our underwriting criteria."
→ Use Opus. This needs careful, deep reasoning — don't rush it.
Best practice

Tips & tricks

A few habits that separate good Claude use from great Claude use. These apply across everything — Chat, Projects, and Skills.

  • Give context, not just a taskInstead of "write an email", try "write an email to a past guest of Southern Ocean Lodge inviting them to return this winter — warm and personal, not promotional." The more context you give, the better the output.
  • Tell Claude who it's writing forSpecify the audience. "Write this for someone who doesn't know Beckons" gives very different results to "write this for a loyal guest who has stayed three times." Claude tailors its language accordingly.
  • Say what you don't wantNegative instructions are just as useful as positive ones. "Keep it under 150 words and don't use exclamation marks" helps Claude avoid common pitfalls before it starts.
  • Invite Claude to ask clarifying questionsEnd your prompt with: "If you need any clarification before you start, please ask me." This gives Claude permission to check assumptions rather than guess — especially useful for complex or ambiguous tasks. You'll often get a better result with one short exchange than by going back and forth on a poor first draft.
  • Ask Claude to improve, not just redoIf the first response isn't quite right, don't just ask again — tell Claude what to change. "That's good but make it 30% shorter and more conversational" is much more effective than starting over.
  • Use Projects for anything recurringIf you find yourself pasting the same documents or instructions into Claude more than twice, create a Project. Upload your SOPs, brand guides, or rate plans once and they're always there.
  • Choose the right model for the taskSonnet for everyday work, Opus for deep analysis and high-stakes outputs, Haiku for quick simple tasks. See the Sonnet / Opus / Haiku section for a full guide.
  • Ask Claude to think out loudFor complex tasks, add "think through this step by step" or "explain your reasoning." You'll often catch issues before they become problems, and the output will be more considered.
  • Verify before you sendClaude is highly capable but not infallible — especially for facts, figures, and dates. Always review outputs before sending externally. Think of Claude as a first-draft writer, not a final authority.
  • Use it to think, not just to writeSome of the best uses of Claude aren't writing tasks at all — "help me think through the pros and cons of these two approaches" or "what am I missing in this plan?" are incredibly valuable.
Do
  • Give Claude a clear role ("act as a revenue analyst")
  • Specify tone, length, and format upfront
  • Share relevant documents and context
  • Invite Claude to ask questions if anything is unclear
  • Choose Opus for complex, high-stakes tasks
  • Iterate — the second response is usually better
Don't
  • Paste guest names, credit card details, or passports
  • Share confidential M&A or board-level documents
  • Send Claude's output without reviewing it first
  • Assume Claude knows Beckons — tell it
  • Install Claude Code without IT approval
Getting great results

How to write a great prompt

Prompting is a skill — but a learnable one. The framework below will help you get better results faster, whatever you're working on.

R

Role

Tell Claude who to be. "Act as a luxury hospitality copywriter with 15 years of experience."

C

Context

Give background. "Beckons is a collection of luxury lodges across Australia, NZ and Fiji."

T

Task

Be specific about what you need. "Write a 200-word winter campaign email for past guests."

F

Format

Specify the output. "Return as a Word document with subject line, preview text, and body."

+Q

Ask for questions

End every prompt with: "If you need any clarification, please ask me before you start."

See it in action

Weak prompt
"Write an email about our winter offer."
→ Claude has no idea who this is for, what the offer is, or what tone to use. The result will be generic.
Strong prompt — RCTF + Q
"You're a luxury travel copywriter. Write a 180-word email for Beckons targeting past guests of The Louise in the Barossa Valley. The goal is to drive bookings for May–July with our new winter tasting menu experience. Tone: warm, personal, evocative — not promotional. Return the email with a subject line and two-sentence preview text. Avoid exclamation marks. If you need any clarification before you start, please ask me."
→ Claude now has everything it needs — and permission to check anything it doesn't.

Useful phrases to keep handy

  • To set the scene"You are [role]. Your audience is [who]. The context is [background]."
  • To specify format"Return this as bullet points / a Word doc / a table / under 200 words."
  • To iterate"That's good. Now make it 30% shorter and more conversational."
  • To pressure-test"What are the three weakest points in this argument? What am I missing?"
  • To get options"Give me three different versions of this — one formal, one casual, one short."
  • To invite clarification"If you need any clarification before you start, please ask me." — Add this to the end of any prompt where the brief is complex or there's room for interpretation. It costs nothing and often saves a round of revision.

Connecting your tools

Connecting Claude to Microsoft 365

Claude can connect directly to your Microsoft 365 environment — Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, OneDrive, and Calendar. Once connected, Claude can find files, draft emails, check your calendar, and more without you having to copy-paste anything.

What you can do once connected

📧

Outlook

Search your inbox, draft replies, find specific emails, and summarise threads without leaving Claude.

📂

SharePoint & OneDrive

Find documents by name or topic, read file contents, and reference them in your prompts.

💬

Teams

Search chat messages, find past discussions, and surface relevant conversations.

📅

Calendar

Check availability, find upcoming meetings, and plan your schedule.

How to connect (takes 2 minutes)

  • Step 1 — open SettingsIn Claude, click your profile icon in the bottom-left corner and select Settings.
  • Step 2 — go to IntegrationsSelect the Integrations tab (also sometimes shown as Connectors).
  • Step 3 — find Microsoft 365Scroll to find Microsoft 365 and click Connect. You'll be prompted to sign in with your Beckons Microsoft account.
  • Step 4 — authorise accessSign in with your Beckons credentials and approve the permissions. Claude will only read what you ask it to — it cannot send emails or modify files without your explicit instruction.
  • Step 5 — try itOpen a new chat and ask: "Find my most recent email about [topic]" or "What meetings do I have this week?"
A note on permissions: Connecting M365 gives Claude read access to your business data within your Microsoft tenant. Claude does not store your emails or documents — it reads them in the moment to answer your question. If you're unsure about connecting, speak with your manager first.
Responsible use

Privacy & safety

Claude is a powerful tool, and like all tools it works best when used responsibly. A few simple guidelines keep Beckons data safe and our guests' trust protected.

Safe to share with Claude
  • Anonymised or fictional examples
  • Internal documents you'd share in a team meeting
  • Rate plans and operational SOPs
  • Brand guidelines and tone of voice docs
  • General business context and background
  • Your own draft copy for review
Never share with Claude
  • Guest passport or ID details
  • Credit card or payment information
  • Sensitive HR or personal data
  • Confidential M&A or board materials
  • Login credentials or passwords
  • Any data marked Confidential or Restricted

Key principles

  • Claude is not a search engineInformation you share in a Claude conversation is not publicly searchable, but treat it with the same care as any business communication.
  • Always review before sendingClaude's outputs are drafts. Before any email, document, or communication goes external, a human should have read and approved it.
  • Don't rely on Claude for factsClaude is very good at reasoning and writing, but it can make factual errors — especially with dates, figures, and recent events. Always verify.
  • When in doubt, askIf you're unsure whether something is appropriate to share with Claude, ask your manager first.

Responsible use

Best practices

Using AI well means using it responsibly. These guidelines apply to everyone at Beckons who uses Claude or any approved AI tool as part of their work.

Connectors and third-party integrations

  • Connectors require approval before useAny integration that connects Claude to another system — including Microsoft 365, SharePoint, or any other third-party service via MCP or similar — must be reviewed and approved by the CFO and the Systems and Projects Manager before use. This applies even if the connected service is itself approved for Company use.
  • What to include in your requestWhen requesting a new Connector, include the name of the integration, what data or systems it will be able to access, confirmation the connected service is approved, and any relevant security or privacy terms covering the integration.
  • Unapproved tools are off-limitsOnly tools on the Beckons Approved AI Tools list may be used for Company business. Personal, trial, or free-tier accounts must not be used for work purposes — no exceptions.

Only use AI for tasks you can verify

  • You remain responsible for the outputAI can be inaccurate, incomplete, or biased. Before anything leaves your hands — an email, a document, a data summary — you must review it yourself. You are accountable for the work regardless of how it was produced.
  • Don't use AI for decisions that require human judgmentAI must not be used to make or automate decisions that are legal, regulatory, employment-related, financial, safety-critical, or customer-facing without meaningful human review and sign-off.
  • AI is not a substitute for professional adviceOutputs are not legal, financial, HR, or medical advice. If a task requires qualified expertise, seek it — don't rely on Claude as a replacement.
  • Verify facts, figures, and datesClaude is highly capable at reasoning and writing but can make factual errors — especially with numbers and recent events. Always check before relying on or sharing any figures.

Prompt responsibly

  • Stay within usage limitsClaude access is provisioned through Company enterprise accounts. Avoid high-volume, automated, or bulk prompting that could affect availability for other team members or breach vendor terms of service.
  • Don't share Protected Information unnecessarilyBefore including any data in a prompt, ask whether you actually need it. Anonymise or summarise where you can. Guest passport or ID details, credit card information, sensitive HR data, confidential M&A materials, login credentials, and anything marked Confidential or Restricted must never be entered into any AI tool.
  • Don't try to work around controlsAttempting to circumvent Beckons or vendor security, access, usage, or content controls is a breach of this policy regardless of intent.
  • Be transparent about AI useYou should be able to explain and stand behind any output where AI played a part. Where disclosure is legally or contractually required, make sure it happens. Don't represent AI-generated content as entirely human-created where that would be misleading.
Do
  • Use only approved tools via your Company account
  • Request approval before connecting any third-party integration
  • Review all outputs before sending or acting on them
  • Anonymise data in prompts wherever possible
  • Keep AI use within reasonable day-to-day limits
  • Report any suspected policy breaches to the Systems and Projects Manager
Don't
  • Use personal or trial accounts for Company work
  • Connect new integrations without CFO and Systems Manager sign-off
  • Rely on AI for legal, financial, or HR decisions
  • Enter Protected Information unless strictly necessary and approved
  • Send or publish AI outputs without human review
  • Attempt to bypass content or usage controls
